# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

BID executive directors and procurement committees across New York City's five boroughs. They are comparing 3-4 contractors side by side for supplemental sanitation contracts. Their context: they manage tight district budgets, answer to boards of directors, and need a contractor who will show up every day and make their streets look clean without drama. They are not browsing — they are evaluating. They ask two questions: "Can this company handle our district?" and "Why choose them over Street Plus?"

## Product Purpose

JAG Maintenance and Cleaning LLC provides street cleaning, power washing, and window cleaning services to Business Improvement Districts throughout NYC's five boroughs. This website exists to establish credibility at a glance, showcase proof of work, and convert BID procurement visitors into contact form submissions, proposal requests, and phone calls.

## Brand Personality

**Dependable, Local, Reliable.**

The brand voice is direct, honest, and workmanlike. Not corporate. Not flashy. Not trying to be a tech company. JAG is the company that shows up, does the work, and has been doing it in these very neighborhoods for years. Brooklyn is not a marketing angle — it is the home base and the identity. The site should feel like the person running it answers the phone himself.

## Anti-references

- Corporate Squarespace-generic. No templated stock photography, no abstract swoosh graphics, no "Solutions" and "Synergy" language.
- Tech-startup aesthetic. No purple gradients, no animated "AI-powered" anything, no chatbot widgets.
- Residential cleaning vibes. No pastel color palettes, no clip-art of smiling maids, no "sparkle" language.
- Street Plus's approach. National, corporate, safe-beige design. JAG is the anti-Street-Plus: local, human, specific.

## Design Principles

1. **Show the work.** Real photography of real crews on real NYC streets. Before/after shots. BID logos. Not stock photos, not icons pretending to be features.
2. **Make it easy to hire us.** Contact form, proposal request, and phone number visible from every scroll position. No hunting for how to reach someone.
3. **Brooklyn is an identity, not a location.** The five-borough reach is a credential. The HQ in Brooklyn is a point of pride. The geography is part of the brand, not a footer afterthought.
4. **Trust through proof, not claims.** BID logos, named testimonials from district directors, specific years of service, specific blocks served. Not "we're the best" — "here's who we work with."
5. **Simple, fast, professional.** The site itself demonstrates competence. Fast load, clean typography, nothing broken. A BID director should think "if their website is this solid, their crews probably are too."

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. The audience includes procurement professionals who may be using assistive technology in government or non-profit settings. Reduced motion respected. Form labels clear and programmatically associated. Phone number tappable on mobile.
